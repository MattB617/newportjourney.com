Matthew Boucher’s Newport X Merritt Parkway – Bridges is a thoughtful and beautifully realized photographic anthology that pays homage to one of the most distinctive roadways in America. As a photographer who has long admired the subtle dialogue between landscape, infrastructure, and history, I found Boucher’s work to be both technically accomplished and quietly reverential. The bridges of the Merritt Parkway are not merely functional structures; they are an open-air gallery of early 20th-century architectural imagination. Constructed largely in the 1930s, each overpass was intentionally designed with its own character, drawing from a range of styles including Art Deco, Neo-Classical, Gothic Revival, and rustic stonework reminiscent of European parkways. Boucher understands this deeply, and his photographs capture the individuality of each structure without losing sight of the unified aesthetic vision that makes the Merritt Parkway so beloved. What stands out most in this anthology is Boucher’s sensitivity to light and perspective. Rather than documenting the bridges as static objects, he frames them as living pieces of the roadway experience. Low angles emphasize the sculptural mass of the concrete arches; oblique compositions reveal repeating geometric patterns in Deco railings; and carefully chosen moments of morning or late-day light draw out textures in stone, steel, and weathered ornament. His images carry a sense of motion and passage, echoing the rhythm of driving beneath these structures one after another. Boucher also demonstrates admirable restraint as a photographer. The series avoids gimmickry or over-processing, allowing the architecture itself to speak. His color palette remains natural and balanced, and his compositions are clean and deliberate. This discipline lends the work an archival quality while still retaining an artistic sensibility. What I appreciate most, however, is the respect the project shows for the Merritt Parkway’s original design philosophy. The parkway was conceived as a scenic drive where infrastructure would harmonize with the surrounding landscape rather than dominate it. Boucher’s photographs honor that idea. Trees frame the bridges, shadows stretch across the roadway, and the structures appear almost as sculptural landmarks emerging organically from the environment. In Newport X Merritt Parkway – Bridges, Matthew Boucher has created more than a catalog of overpasses.
